üî• Critical Differences from Normal Workflows:

  • Time-Critical: 4-hour SLA from incident to resolution - every minute counts
  • Authority Concentration: Emergency Approver has override authority on all decisions
  • Bypass Everything: All normal checks, policies, and stages are bypassed
  • Parallel Execution: Multiple activities run simultaneously to save time
  • War Room Coordination: Dedicated team assembled for real-time collaboration
  • Direct to Production: Skip staging, canary, and gradual rollouts
  • Executive Visibility: Leadership informed throughout, accountable for escalations

‚ö° Emergency Protocols:

  • Rollback First Option: Can stabilize system before attempting fix
  • Minimal Testing: Only critical/smoke tests - 30 minutes maximum
  • Single Approver: One emergency approver can override all normal approvals
  • Customer Communication: Support team actively manages customer expectations
  • Backporting: Fix must be propagated to all active branches after resolution

üìä Key Metrics:

  • Detection to War Room: < 15 minutes
  • Fix Development: 30-90 minutes
  • Testing: 30 minutes maximum
  • Total Resolution: < 4 hours
